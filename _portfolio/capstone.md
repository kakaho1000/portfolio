### Goal 
Integrate a 24V robotic hand with 7.2V prosthetic parts to operate the hand as a transradial, myoelectric prosthetic device.

### Process
I led stakeholder consultations with a local prosthetics clinic to gain more insight on typical user needs, the industry, and its technology. During development, I performed hardware testing, ensuring the product does not exceed the prosthetic parts' current limits. For the final prototype, I designed and 3D printed a mount for the device.

### Results
The final prototype involved an electrode armband that users can attach to their forearm to control the device with flexion and extension. Every interested passerby was able to successfully control the device, and the client was very satisfied with the delivered product.

### Retrospection
As a mechanical engineering student not specializing in mechatronics or biomed, this project was a struggle, but also a fantastic learning opportunity. Through debugging Modbus RTU, designing a slip ring PCB, and creating a power delivery system with buck converters, I learned so much about electrical and software integration.
