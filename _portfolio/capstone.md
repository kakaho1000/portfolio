### Goal 
Integrate a 24V industrial robotic hand with 7.2V prosthetic parts to operate the hand as a transradial, myoelectric prosthetic device.

### Process
In the early stages, I was in charge of leading stakeholder consultations with a local prosthetics clinic to gain more insight on typical user needs, the industry, and its technology. I developed knowledge on different prosthetics companies, the typical control algorithms for myoelectric prosthetics, and the parts that were appropriate to purchase for our project.

During development, I performed testing on the robotic hand, troubleshooting with the client remotely, and ensuring the final product does not exceed the prosthetic parts' current limits. I also helped a teammate troubleshoot ModBus signals using a serial sniffer. For demonstration, I designed and 3D printed a mount for the device.

### Results
The final project involved an electrode armband for demonstration at Design & Innovation Day. Users can select between two modes: direct control, where wrist flexion and extension lead to activation and deactivation of a predetermined gesture, or proportional control, where the amount the gesture is activated depends on the strength of the muscles activated.

### Retrospection
As this project involved integrating existing products, there was little room for mechanical design, and difficulties that arose were moreso related to electrical troubleshooting, testing, and verification.
